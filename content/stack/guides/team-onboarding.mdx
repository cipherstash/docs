# Team onboarding

Every developer on your team gets their own device and client when they initialize CipherStash.
This means every encrypt, decrypt, and secret access operation is traceable to a specific developer.

## How team access works

When a developer runs `npx @cipherstash/stack init`, CipherStash creates:

- A **device** tied to that developer's user account and machine
- A **client** associated with the device, used for cryptographic operations
- Automatic access to the **default keyset** in your workspace

No credentials are shared between developers.
Each person has their own identity, and access can be granted or revoked individually.

## Adding a team member

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Invite them to your organization

    In the [CipherStash Dashboard](https://dashboard.cipherstash.com), navigate to **Members** in the organization sidebar.

    Add the new team member's email address.
    They will receive an invitation to create a CipherStash account (or join with an existing one).
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Grant workspace access

    Once they accept the invitation, grant them access to the relevant workspace(s) in **Settings** for your workspace.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Developer runs init

    The new team member runs the init command on their machine:

    ```bash
    npx @cipherstash/stack init
    ```

    This authenticates them via the browser, creates their unique device and client, and grants access to the default keyset.

    They are now ready to develop locally with full CipherStash functionality — no environment variables needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing team access

### Viewing active clients

Navigate to **Clients** in the Dashboard to see all active clients, including device-backed clients created by `npx @cipherstash/stack init`.
Each client shows which developer and device it belongs to.

### Revoking a developer's access

To remove a developer's access:

1. Remove their client from keyset access in the Dashboard
2. Remove them from the workspace
3. Optionally, remove them from the organization

Revoked clients can no longer perform encrypt, decrypt, or secret operations.

### Multiple machines

If a developer works on more than one machine (e.g., a laptop and a desktop), they run `npx @cipherstash/stack init` on each machine.
Each machine gets its own device and client.
This is by design — you can revoke access per device without affecting their other machines.

## Relationship to production

Team devices are for **local development only**.
Production environments use separate [machine clients](/stack/guides/going-to-production) configured with environment variables.

This separation ensures that:
- Developer credentials never appear in production infrastructure
- Production access is controlled independently from developer access
- Revoking a developer's device does not affect production systems
